What is Naawi-Oodena

Naawi-Oodena is a 160 acre site developed by the Canada Lands Company and Treaty One Nation. The name means "center of the heart and community" in Anishinaabe. It combines First Nations traditional values and culture with modern city planning like zoning and streets. The plan includes affordable and market-rate housing, a Cultural Centre, public gathering place, and parks and trails. There are also fields for sports, playgrounds, and walking and biking trails. Indigenous culture is reflected through street and park names, art styles, and features.

Where it's located?

Naawi-Oodena is located at the former Kapyong Barracks site in Winnipeg. This 160-acre property was home to the Canadian military's Fort Osborne Barracks until June 2004. After years of negotiations, the land was transferred to Treaty One Nation on August 30, 2019. The development is situated at the corner of Kenaston Boulevard and Grant Avenue.
